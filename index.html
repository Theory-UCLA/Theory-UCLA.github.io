About this Group

A lot of advances in theoretical computer sciences stem from techniques that belong to a common toolbox. Our hope is to equip you, a theorist, with this toolbox. We start by introducing fundamental mathematical concepts such as linear algebra, graph theory, probability theory, information theory, and more. We will also demonstrate their applicability towards solving a variety of combinatorial problems. The examples we'll discuss will be concise but rigorous and might show unexpected links between different problems.

Topics Outline

Refer to this document for a more detailed list of topics.

  • Dimension arguments and linear algebra methods
  • Random walks and spectral graph theory
  • Information theory
  • The Probabilistic Method
  • Quantum computing
