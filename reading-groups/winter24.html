About

Zero-knowledge proofs (ZKPs) have the remarkable ability to be convincing and yield nothing beyond the validity of an assertion. It turns out the ZKPs exist for all languages in NP (assuming one-way functions exist). Recently, ZKPs have gained a lot of traction because of their applications in blockchains and authentication systems.

Topics

  • Definitions of perfect, statistical and computational zero-knowledge
  • Soundness Amplification
  • Constant-Round Zero-Knowledge
  • Non-Interactive Zero-Knowledge

Resources

  • Alex Lombardi's MIT course on Cryptographic Proofs Fall 2019
  • Section 5 of Yehuda Lindell's tutorial on simulation proofs How To Simulate It
  • Jonathan Katz's course on Advanced Topics in Cryptography
  • Chapter 4 of Oded Goldreich's textbook on Foundations of Cryptography - Volume 1
